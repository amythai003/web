Amy Asks

+ What is "Lazy Man's Yoga" in Thai Massage?

Thai massage is often called "Lazy Man's Yoga" because the therapist moves your body into various yoga-like positions. This provides all the benefits of deep stretching and joint mobilization without you having to do any of the work.

+ How does the "Sen Sib" energy system work?

Based on the concept of "Sen Sib" or 10 energy lines, Thai massage applies pressure to specific points to clear blockages. This restores the flow of vital energy throughout the body, promoting holistic healing beyond just muscle relief.

+ Can Thai massage help detoxify the body?

Yes! The rhythmic pressing and stretching techniques help stimulate the lymphatic system. This aids the body in flushing out toxins and metabolic waste more efficiently, which is why drinking water after a session is highly recommended.

+ What is the history behind this tradition?

It is a 2,500-year-old sacred art with roots in Indian Ayurvedic medicine and Chinese healing. Founded by Jivaka Kumar Bhaccha, a contemporary of the Buddha, it remains one of the oldest active healing traditions in the world.

+ Does Thai massage help with stress and anxiety?

Beyond physical relief, the meditative rhythm of Thai massage lowers cortisol (the stress hormone) and boosts serotonin. It is a powerful tool for reducing anxiety and helping the nervous system switch into a state of deep rest.
